Methodology & data sources

Box office and film metadata come from the TMDB API, filtered to films with a reported worldwide gross above $1 million USD. Dog-death confidence scores come from the DoesTheDogDie.com API (free tier, attribution required), filtered to films with at least 20 community votes on the "dogs dying" topic. Films with no TMDB revenue reported are excluded.

Each point represents one film. Colour encodes the dog-death confidence score: red (≥70%) means the community is confident a dog dies; blue (≤30%) means they're confident it doesn't; violet is uncertain. X-axis shows worldwide box office gross; Y-axis shows the percentage of voters who said "yes, the dog dies." Log scale on X is recommended as revenue is heavily right-skewed. The Pearson r value measures linear correlation between log₁₀(revenue) and dog-death percentage.

Data refreshes every Sunday via GitHub Actions. Download the dataset (JSON).
